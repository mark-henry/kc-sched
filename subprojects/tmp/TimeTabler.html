TimeTabler

TimeTabler is a non-free application available for download at www.timetabler.com.  They claim it is used in over 70 countries and that it’s extremely intuitive to use.  “It requires no knowledge of computers.”  It was also designed and developed by professionals in the time-tabling industry.  Keith Johnson is one of the developers who are continuously making improvements (he has written a few books on the notorious “timetabling” problem).

The Good:

  • Deals with shared classroom problems
  • Scheduling of part-time teachers
  • Handles split-site schools
  • Self-checking (100% guarantee that the schedule will work on the first day of class)
  • By use of certain algorithms, it will suggest subsequent decisions for optimal efficiency
    • Lists these in the form of priorities
    • Ex) it will recommend which course you should place where and who should teach it based on current progress
  • Will generate schedule interactively, automatically, or both
  • Help screens at each phase (concrete examples, and even movies)
  • Supports printing from different perspectives 
    • Overall schedule 
    • Individual schedules
    • Course schedules
  • Generates HTML version of schedule

The Bad:

  • Cannot export to excel, doc, or other popular formats
  • Weak GUI

Missing Features:

  • No import capability to eliminate data entry
  • No database interactivity, must be manually entered
