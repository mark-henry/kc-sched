1.4. Impacts

The introduction of this automated scheduling tool is expected to have the following positive impacts:

a.       decrease the time and effort it takes to put together a working schedule

b.      elimination of the possibility of errors within a department’s schedule

c.       simplify the communication between faculty members when coordinating to create the schedule

Potential negative impacts may occur, such as dependency on network or internet access to work on the schedule. Any bug in the software that results in a problematic schedule could severely discredit the developers, and leave the scheduler’s distrustful of scheduling software. If the software is engineered well however, the positive potential impacts far outweigh the negative.
